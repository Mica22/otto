Financial Technology
Payments & Transactions

Post-trade processing must be handled efficiently for security, as capital markets are volatile. Sending, receiving, and verifying funds at an international level is usually expensive and time-consuming. As blockchain-based payments cannot be centralized, they offer instantaneous transactions between the contractual parties without the need for third parties. The peer-to-peer verification network, as well as cryptographically secure end-to-end payments, further secure the transaction. Furthermore, smart contracts can replace usually expensive customer reward programs, such as gift cards, ticketing, and voucher prepaid channels. These can be easier, quicker, and cheaper to handle with blockchain-based processors.


Information Technology
Smart Systems & Data Storage

The technology allows multiple devices to connect to each other securely rather than needing one central cloud server, reducing the risk of device spoofing and impersonation. A blockchain-based service can function as an immutable record of evidence, which can be beneficial both for supply chain management and asset identification. Typically, encrypted public ledgers are used to store registries of personal data, such as medical documentation, marriage certificates, and education records. These records are immutable,their authenticity can be verified and transparency asserted.


Digital Media
Content Distribution & Intellectual Property

By implementing blockchain-based processors, users can directly control the distribution and monetization of their works rather than relying on third parties. This platform provides data security and attribution of provenance and attribution of royalties, as well as the ability to calculate and automate licensing and royalty payments through blockchain notarization.
